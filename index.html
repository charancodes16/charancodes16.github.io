My Perspective on Design

Design is more than just how a product looks on the surface. Good design involves considering all aspects of the product to ensure a positive user experience. One important aspect is the sensory stimuli that are present in the interface. To achieve this, I must balance both aesthetics and functionality, taking into account the needs of the user. Responsible design practices involve tapping into people's deep emotions, which can have a significant impact on their lives. The goal of design is not just to create something that looks good, but also to create something that works well and makes people feel good. So that should create social impact and help businesses achieve their goals.

My journey in the world of Financial technology

My love for financial technology and trading began during the pandemic. As an product designer intern at Freecharge, I was introduced to Neo-banking, lending products, mutual funds, and equity markets. It was a thrilling experience to explore financial disciplines and wealth management at such a young age.

After my internship, I spent time learning more about equity markets and payment inclusions. I wasn't afraid to take risks and come up with crazy ideas for fintech, even if they failed. It was all about trusting the process and improving my learning curve.

Eventually, I became a founding designer at an Angel startup on Capital markets where I worked closely with the founding members. This experience taught me the real faces of the financial world, including the complexities of equity markets. We ideated, designed, and built business models and functionalities, but unfortunately, Despite achieving a large market fit, the startup on hold and inactive due to a problem with the business model in relation to RBI regulations.

Now, as a product design intern at HDFC Bank, one of the largest banks in India, I'm continuing to pursue my passion for financial inclusion and implementing my ideas. I believe that design plays a crucial role in building trust in the financial world, and I'm excited to contribute to this mission through my work at HDFC Bank. I'm looking forward to making a positive impact on people's lives in millions by designing products that are user-friendly, intuitive, and trustworthy.

Persuasive Design

Tristan Harris, a former Google design ethicist and co-founder of the Center for Humane Technology, is a Stanford alumnus who has been fighting for good digital inclusion in our socially connected world. His work is focused on the ways in which data enhancements and persuasive modifications of behavior captivate human attention and time, often at the expense of our well-being.

From 2021, I became interested in persuasive design after learning about Harris's work. I researched how to persuade people and how it works, particularly in terms of how data is connected and how algorithms are used to captivate audiences for the benefit of organizations' business growth and profit goals. Through case studies and analysis of persuasive design examples and architecture, I learned about the gaps in the human brain that make us susceptible to these techniques.

I drew inspiration from the work of Professor BJ Fogg's Prompt design and Daniel Kahneman's resources, among others. Using these resources, I ideated how persuasive design works in practice and created a presentation demonstrating its effectiveness. Overall, my research into persuasive design has helped me understand how our digital world is designed to captivate and how we can use this knowledge to promote more ethical and human-centered technology.
